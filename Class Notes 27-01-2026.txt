**Type Promotion** in Java is the process by which **smaller data types are automatically converted into larger data types** during expressions or method calls.


### ðŸ”¹ Type Promotion

---

## **Rule 1: byte, short, char â†’ int**

Even if both operands are `byte`, the result is `int`.

# **Arithmetic Operator Precedence in Java**
============================================

---

##What Is Operator Precedence?**

**Operator precedence** determines **which operator is evaluated first** when multiple operators appear in the same expression **without parentheses**.

ðŸ‘‰ If precedence is the same, **associativity** decides the order.

---

## Why Operator Precedence Matters**

Consider:

int result = 10 + 5 * 2;



âŒ Wrong assumption:

(10 + 5) * 2 = 30


âœ… Actual evaluation:
10 + (5 * 2) = 20



Because `*` has **higher precedence** than `+`.

---

##Arithmetic Operator Precedence Order (High â†’ Low)**

| Precedence  | Operators     | Description                       |
| ----------- | ------------- | --------------------------------- |
| 1 (Highest) | `()`          | Parentheses                       |
| 2           | `++`, `--`    | Unary increment / decrement       |
| 3           | `*`, `/`, `%` | Multiplication, Division, Modulus |
| 4           | `+`, `-`      | Addition, Subtraction             |
| 5 (Lowest)  | `=`           | Assignment                        |

ðŸ“Œ **Unary operators are evaluated before arithmetic operators.**


##Step-by-Step Evaluation Examples**

---

### ðŸ”¹ Example 1

int x = 10 + 5 * 2;



Evaluation:


5 * 2 = 10

10 + 10 = 20



---

### ðŸ”¹ Example 2


int x = (10 + 5) * 2;



Evaluation:



(10 + 5) = 15

15 * 2 = 30



---

### ðŸ”¹ Example 3

int x = 20 / 5 * 2;



Same precedence â†’ left to right:



20 / 5 = 4

4 * 2 = 8



### ðŸ”¹ Example 4

int x = 5;

int y = x++ + ++x;



Step-by-step:


x++ â†’ use 5, then x = 6

++x â†’ x becomes 7

y = 5 + 7 = 12



---

### ðŸ”¹ Example 5


int x = 5;

System.out.println(++x * 2);





++x â†’ 6

6 * 2 = 12



---

## Modulus `%` Precedence**

`%` has **same precedence as `*` and `/`**

---

### ðŸ”¹ Example 6


System.out.println(10 + 5 % 3);


5 % 3 = 2

10 + 2 = 12


---

## Mixing Data Types & Precedence**

Precedence applies **before type promotion**.

---

### ðŸ”¹ Example 7


System.out.println(10 + 20 * 3.0);



Evaluation:


20 * 3.0 = 60.0

10 + 60.0 = 70.0



---

##Common Tricky Expressions**

---

### ðŸ”¹ Example 8


System.out.println(100 / 10 * 10);





100 / 10 = 10

10 * 10 = 100



---

### ðŸ”¹ Example 9


System.out.println(100 / (10 * 10));





10 * 10 = 100

100 / 100 = 1



---

##Operator Precedence vs Parentheses**

ðŸ“Œ **Parentheses ALWAYS win**



int x = 10 + 2 * 3;     // 16

int y = (10 + 2) * 3;  // 36

---

##Summary**

* Precedence decides evaluation order
* Parentheses override precedence
* Same precedence â†’ left to right
* Unary operators are evaluated first
* Modulus has the same precedence as * and /
